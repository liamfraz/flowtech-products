# Client Onboarding Email Templates

Three ready-to-send emails for the first week of every new engagement. Customise the bracketed fields and send.

---

## Email 1: Project Kickoff

**Subject:** Welcome aboard — here's what happens next 🚀

**Send when:** Immediately after the contract is signed and deposit is received.

---

Hi [Client First Name],

Thank you for choosing [Your Business Name] — I'm excited to get started on [project name].

Here's a quick overview of what happens next:

**This week:**
1. You'll receive a Client Questionnaire (if you haven't already) — please complete it by [date]
2. I'll prepare the project brief based on your responses
3. We'll schedule our kickoff call

**Project details:**
- **Start date:** [Date]
- **Estimated delivery:** [Date]
- **Your point of contact:** [Your Name] — [your email]

**How we'll communicate:**
- Day-to-day updates: [Email / Slack / Teams]
- Scheduled check-ins: [Weekly / fortnightly] via [Zoom / Google Meet]
- File sharing: [Google Drive / Dropbox / your preferred tool]

If you have any questions before our kickoff call, just reply to this email.

Looking forward to building something great together.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Business Name]
[Your Phone Number]

## Email 2: Access & Credentials Request

**Subject:** Quick request — access and credentials needed

**Send when:** 1–2 days after the kickoff email, or when you're ready to begin technical work.

---

Hi [Client First Name],

Before I dive into the project, I'll need access to a few accounts and resources. Could you provide the following by [date]?

### Accounts & Logins
- [ ] Website / CMS admin access (URL + credentials)
- [ ] Hosting / server access (cPanel, SSH, or provider login)
- [ ] Domain registrar access (if DNS changes are needed)
- [ ] Analytics (Google Analytics, Search Console)
- [ ] Social media accounts (if applicable)

### Brand Assets
- [ ] Logo files (SVG or PNG with transparent background)
- [ ] Brand guidelines document (colours, fonts, tone of voice)
- [ ] Existing photography or image library
- [ ] Copy / content for key pages (if available)

### Third-Party Services
- [ ] Payment processor credentials (Stripe, PayPal, etc.)
- [ ] Email marketing platform (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.)
- [ ] CRM access (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.)
- [ ] Any API keys or integration credentials

**Security note:** Please share credentials through a secure method — I recommend [1Password shared vault / Bitwarden Send / a secure link]. Please don't send passwords in plain email.

Let me know if any of these don't apply or if you need help setting anything up.

Thanks,
[Your Name]

## Email 3: First Meeting Agenda

**Subject:** Kickoff call agenda — [Date] at [Time]

**Send when:** 24 hours before the scheduled kickoff meeting.

---

Hi [Client First Name],

Looking forward to our kickoff call tomorrow. Here's what we'll cover:

### Meeting Details
- **Date:** [Date]
- **Time:** [Time] ([Timezone])
- **Duration:** 45–60 minutes
- **Link:** [Zoom / Google Meet link]

### Agenda

**1. Introductions & Roles (5 min)**
- Who's involved from your side and what their role is
- How I'll be working with your team

**2. Project Goals & Priorities (10 min)**
- Confirm the primary objectives
- Discuss success metrics — how will we know this project succeeded?

**3. Questionnaire Review (10 min)**
- Walk through your responses
- Clarify any open questions

**4. Scope & Deliverables Walkthrough (10 min)**
- Confirm what's in scope (and what's out)
- Review the deliverables list and formats

**5. Timeline & Milestones (10 min)**
- Review the project schedule
- Confirm key dates and dependencies
- Agree on feedback turnaround expectations

**6. Communication & Tools (5 min)**
- Confirm preferred channels and tools
- Set expectations for response times

**7. Open Questions & Next Steps (5 min)**
- Anything we haven't covered
- Action items for both sides

### Before the Call
Please have the following ready (if possible):
- [ ] Completed client questionnaire
- [ ] Access credentials (from my previous email)
- [ ] Any reference material or inspiration examples

See you tomorrow!

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Business Name]

## Tips for Using These Templates

- **Personalise every email.** Replace all bracketed fields. Generic onboarding emails undermine the premium experience you're selling.
- **Send promptly.** The first 48 hours after signing set the tone for the entire project. Fast, organised communication builds immediate confidence.
- **Save as snippets.** Store these in your email client (Gmail templates, TextExpander, etc.) so you can fire them off in under 2 minutes.
- **Adapt to your niche.** If you're a developer, the access request email might need more technical fields. If you're a consultant, swap technical access for strategic documents.
