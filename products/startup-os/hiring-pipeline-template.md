# Hiring Pipeline Template

Three templates in one: a job description framework, an interview scorecard, and an offer letter template. Designed for early-stage startups making their first 1–20 hires.

---

# Part 1: Job Description Template

## [Job Title]

**Company:** [Company Name]
**Location:** [Remote / City, Country / Hybrid]
**Type:** [Full-time / Part-time / Contract]
**Compensation:** [Salary range or "Competitive + equity"]
**Reports To:** [Role / Name]

---

### About [Company Name]

[2–3 sentences about what your company does, the problem you're solving, and why it matters. Be specific — "We're disrupting X" means nothing. What do you actually build?]

> Example: We build invoicing software for freelancers who hate invoicing. 4,000 users, profitable, bootstrapped. We ship fast and talk to customers every day.

### About the Role

[What will this person actually do day-to-day? Be honest. If the role involves 40% customer support, say so.]

> Example: You'll own the entire frontend — from the React codebase to the design system. In your first month, you'll ship our new dashboard. By month three, you'll be leading our first hire into the frontend team.

### What You'll Do

- [Responsibility 1 — e.g., Build and maintain customer-facing features in React/TypeScript]
- [Responsibility 2 — e.g., Own the CI/CD pipeline and deploy process]
- [Responsibility 3 — e.g., Participate in weekly user calls to inform product decisions]
- [Responsibility 4 — e.g., Review PRs and mentor junior developers as the team grows]
- [Responsibility 5 — e.g., Collaborate directly with the founder on product roadmap]

### What We're Looking For

- [Requirement 1 — e.g., 3+ years of production experience with React and TypeScript]
- [Requirement 2 — e.g., You've shipped something end-to-end that real users depend on]
- [Requirement 3 — e.g., Comfortable working autonomously with minimal hand-holding]
- [Requirement 4 — e.g., Strong written communication — we're async-first]
- [Requirement 5 — e.g., Bonus: experience with Stripe, Postgres, or Tailwind]

### What We Offer

- [Benefit 1 — e.g., $120K–$150K base + 0.5–1.0% equity]
- [Benefit 2 — e.g., Fully remote with async-first culture]
- [Benefit 3 — e.g., $2,000/year learning & tools budget]
- [Benefit 4 — e.g., 4 weeks PTO + your local public holidays]
- [Benefit 5 — e.g., Small team, big ownership — your work ships to users weekly]

### How to Apply

[e.g., Send your resume and a short note about something you've built to hiring@company.com. No cover letter needed — just tell us what you're proud of.]

## Tips for Founders

- **Job descriptions:** Be honest about the chaos. Early-stage roles are messy — the right candidates find that exciting.
- **Scorecards:** Fill them out immediately after the interview, before discussing with others. First impressions decay fast.
- **Offer letters:** Move fast. A 48-hour delay after final interview costs you candidates. Have the template ready to go.
- **Equity:** Be transparent about dilution, vesting, and exercise windows. Candidates who don't understand equity make bad decisions.
- **Reference checks:** Always call at least 2 references. Ask: "Would you hire them again?" — the pause tells you everything.
