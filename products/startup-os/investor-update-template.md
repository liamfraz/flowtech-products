## Highlights

What went well this month. Be specific — "good progress" means nothing.

1. **[e.g., Closed first enterprise customer]** — [Company Name] signed a $2,400/yr annual contract after a 3-week pilot. They're in [industry] with [X] employees. This validates our pricing for mid-market.

2. **[e.g., Launched self-serve onboarding]** — New users can now get to value without a demo call. Early data: 45% of signups complete setup vs 22% last month.

3. **[e.g., Featured in [Publication/Newsletter]]** — Drove 1,200 unique visitors and 84 signups in 48 hours. Best organic channel to date.

---

## Lowlights

What didn't go well. This is the most important section — it builds trust and helps your investors help you.

1. **[e.g., Churn spiked to 8%]** — Root cause: users who signed up from the ProductHunt launch had low intent. Cohort analysis shows organic signups churn at 3%. We're tightening our onboarding qualifier.

2. **[e.g., Engineering hire fell through]** — Candidate accepted a competing offer. Back to sourcing. This delays the API launch by ~3 weeks.

3. **[e.g., Paid acquisition experiment failed]** — Spent $2,100 on Google Ads with a $210 CAC (target: $50). Pausing paid until we nail organic conversion.

## Asks

Be specific. Investors can't help with vague requests. "Introductions to enterprise buyers" is useless. "An intro to [Specific Person] at [Specific Company] because [Specific Reason]" gets results.

1. **[e.g., Intro request]** — Looking for an introduction to [Person, Company]. They're in our ICP and we've seen [signal — e.g., they posted about this problem on LinkedIn].

2. **[e.g., Hiring]** — Searching for a senior full-stack engineer (React/Node). If you know anyone who's built SaaS products at early-stage, please send them our way: [Link to job post].

3. **[e.g., Advice]** — We're debating whether to pursue SOC 2 now or wait until we have enterprise pipeline. Would love 15 minutes with anyone who's navigated this decision.

## Tips for Founders

- **Send it even when things are bad.** Radio silence destroys investor trust faster than bad news.
- **Keep the same format every month.** Investors compare updates over time — consistency makes it easy.
- **Write it in 30 minutes.** If it takes longer, you're overthinking it. Data + honesty + asks = done.
- **BCC your investors** or use a tool like Visible.vc. Don't CC — investors don't need to see each other's replies.
- **Include your spouse/co-founder.** They should see the same numbers you're sharing externally.
- **Save every update** in a running document. It becomes an invaluable company history.
