## Impact/Effort Scoring Matrix

Score every initiative before adding it to the roadmap. This prevents "loudest voice wins" prioritisation.

### Scoring Guide

**Impact (1–5):**

| Score | Meaning | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Game-changing — directly drives core metric | Feature that 80% of churned users requested |
| 4 | High — meaningful improvement to key metric | Reduces onboarding time by 50% |
| 3 | Medium — useful but not transformative | New export format, minor UX improvement |
| 2 | Low — nice-to-have, limited reach | Admin-only feature, edge case fix |
| 1 | Minimal — no measurable impact expected | Cosmetic change, internal tooling polish |

**Effort (1–5):**

| Score | Meaning | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Trivial — hours, single person | Copy change, config update, feature flag |
| 2 | Small — 1–3 days, one person | New API endpoint, simple UI component |
| 3 | Medium — 1–2 weeks, 1–2 people | New feature with backend + frontend work |
| 4 | Large — 2–4 weeks, small team | Major feature, new integration, migration |
| 5 | Massive — 4+ weeks, cross-team | Platform rewrite, new product surface |

**Priority Score = Impact / Effort** (higher is better)

## Tips for Founders

- **One theme per quarter.** If everything is a priority, nothing is. The theme is your filter.
- **Score before committing.** Gut-feel prioritisation creates resentment. Numbers create alignment.
- **Say no loudly.** The "Cut" section is as important as the "Now" section. Teams need permission to not do things.
- **Ship dates are commitments.** If you put a date on it, you're accountable. Leave dates blank for backlog items.
- **Review weekly, rewrite quarterly.** The roadmap is a living document, not a contract.
- **Share it externally.** Send a simplified Now/Next/Later view to your investors and advisors. Transparency builds trust.
