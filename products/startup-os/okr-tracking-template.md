## How OKRs Work

- **Objective**: A qualitative goal. Ambitious, inspiring, time-bound to this quarter.
- **Key Result**: A measurable outcome that proves the objective is being met. 2–4 per objective.
- **Scoring**: At quarter-end, score each KR from 0.0 to 1.0. Average the KR scores to get the objective score.
  - **0.7–1.0** = On track / delivered
  - **0.4–0.6** = Progress made, fell short
  - **0.0–0.3** = Failed to move the needle

> **Rule of thumb:** If you consistently score 1.0 on everything, your OKRs aren't ambitious enough.

## Tips for Founders

- **Keep it small.** 2–3 company objectives max. More than that and nothing is a priority.
- **Separate OKRs from BAU.** OKRs are stretch goals, not your to-do list. Day-to-day work still happens.
- **Check in monthly**, not just at quarter-end. A mid-quarter course correction beats a post-mortem.
- **Make them visible.** Pin OKRs in Slack, Notion, or wherever your team works daily.
- **Celebrate 0.7.** A 0.7 score means you aimed high and delivered meaningfully. That's the sweet spot.
