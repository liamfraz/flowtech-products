## When to Choose Each Option

### Choose a Startup OS template if:
- You're a solo founder or team of 2–4
- You want all operational templates in one purchase
- You switch between tools (Notion this month, Linear next month) and need portability
- You care about investor-ready formats
- You want to spend 30 minutes setting up, not a full day

### Choose Notion templates if:
- Your entire team already lives in Notion
- You want connected databases (OKRs linked to tasks linked to projects)
- You value beautiful UI and real-time collaboration
- You're comfortable with Notion's learning curve
- You don't plan to leave Notion anytime soon

### Choose DIY spreadsheets if:
- You're pre-revenue and truly cannot spend $29
- You only need one or two operational areas (e.g., just burn rate tracking)
- You enjoy building systems and consider it thinking time
- Your team is spreadsheet-native (finance backgrounds)

## The Hidden Cost of "Free"

Here's what most founders don't calculate: the time cost of building operational templates from scratch.

If it takes you 6 hours to build a decent set of standup notes, OKR trackers, hiring scorecards, investor updates, product roadmaps, burn rate models, and metrics dashboards — and your time is worth even $50/hour — that's $300 of your time to save $29.

The maths gets worse when you factor in iteration. Your first version of an investor update template won't match what VCs expect. Your first OKR tracker will miss the scoring nuances. You'll rebuild each template 2–3 times before it's solid.

This is why template packs exist. Someone has already done the iterations.

## What Good Startup Operations Look Like

Regardless of which tool you choose, here's what your operational stack should cover by the time you have 3+ people:

1. **Weekly rhythm** — A standup or sync framework that takes under 30 minutes and produces actionable next steps
2. **Goal tracking** — OKRs or equivalent, reviewed quarterly, scored honestly (0.7 is good; 1.0 means you sandbagged)
3. **Hiring process** — Structured scorecards so you're evaluating candidates consistently, not vibes-based
4. **Investor communication** — Monthly or quarterly updates in a format that's scannable in 2 minutes
5. **Product prioritisation** — A framework (impact/effort, RICE, whatever) so you're not just building whatever the loudest customer asked for
6. **Financial visibility** — Know your burn rate, runway, and what happens if revenue dips 20%
7. **Metrics tracking** — The 5–7 numbers that actually tell you if the business is working

If your current setup doesn't cover all seven, you have gaps that will bite you.
